For the last 2-3 days the news of Taliban’s new Deputy Prime Minister Abdul Ghani Baradar went viral particularly in India and Pakistan. According to a hoard of handles, Baradar was allegedly killed either by COVID-19 or in a bomb attack on Monday.

The news was spread massively in Indian and Pakistani circles. An image of Baradar with the headline and Al-Jazeera’s logo was also circulated along with this claim .

# Aljazira:



According to a Taliban spokesman, Mullah Baradar, the Taliban deputy prime minister, was killed in an inter-team confrontation over the selection of cabinet members.



The accuracy of this news has not been confirmed by other official sources from the Taliban. pic.twitter.com/F4aY7TiXDT — Hanif Rezaie (@hanifR77360437) September 13, 2021 Image posted by an user https://twitter.com/PanjshirObserv/status/1437199064127328256 Claim posted by Panjshir Observer

Similar claim was posted by hundred of handles

Fact-Check :

Upon trying to look for this claim on Al Jazeera’s website, we could not find any such news there.

This claim was then addressed by the Taliban themselves when they themselves issued a statement that this is false news. Mullah Baradar himself released a statement calling this claim false.

Therefore, this claim is fake.
