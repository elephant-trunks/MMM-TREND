As the Indian government is trying hard to bring back fugitive businessman Vijay Mallya, a message going viral on social media claims that Congress leader Kapil Sibal moved a petition in the London High Court to stop his extradition. This claim has been put up by Facebook page 'NAMO' which has more than 45 lakh followers.

The post had been shared more than 9,700 times at the time of filing this report. Users are calling Sibal "anti-national" and even giving him death threats.

India Today's Anti Fake News War Room (AFWA) has found this claim to be totally false. The original Facebook post can be seen here.

We found no media report about Sibal's role in the extradition of Mallya. Speaking to India Today, Sibal said, This is absolutely false, I have never represented Mallya."

We found that this post was picked from a report on website fakingnews.com which clearly states it is a satire website. The disclaimer on the website reads, "Content of this website, unless categorised as 'Editorial' is a work of fiction."

The screenshot of the post has been mischievously put up in a way that it looks like real news.
